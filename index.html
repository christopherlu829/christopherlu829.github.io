As I have ventured through my educational career, every year I find myself meeting influential individuals, learning different perspectives, and questioning science and the world around me. Driven by something that previously caused me pain and regret, I have aligned my professional and personal aspirations to make the world a better place by supporting and fighting for the individuals who lost opportunity.

From 2015-2018, I battled both Graves' Disease and Graves' Opthalmology suffering physically, emotionally, and academically. Ever since I have been in remission continuously taking medication knowing that there is no existing 'cure' for Graves'. Living in this fearful state knowing that every little symptom might indicate a change in my thyroid activity and thus a quick reversal of all the medical progress I have made is something millions internationally also have in their minds. My drive is to discover the 'cure' or at the very least, help individuals suffering from medical illnesses be able to see the light at the end of the tunnel and discover passion from pain and regret.

Approaching this goal, I am majoring in Biomedical Engineering with a concentration in Cell and Tissue Engineering at the University of Rochester and minoring in Business- to better understand both the medical and business side of academic and industry-based scientific research. I am the business manager of the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers with the hope of both applying the skills I have learned in my minor and also increasing Asian students' interest in scientific research and project-based discovery at a younger age. Finally, I am a member of my university’s Medallion Leadership Society where after completing 50+ hours of workshops and seminars, I now lead new endeavors with organizations like NEUO, further develop my leadership skills, and teach the multifaceted aspects of leadership to students across the university.

In my free time, my interests and hobbies range include building my investment portfolio, playing sports with friends, and photography. I would love to connect with others in the pharmaceuticals sciences, biomedical sciences, engineering, and biotechnology fields, please feel free to email me at christopherlu829@gmail.com!
